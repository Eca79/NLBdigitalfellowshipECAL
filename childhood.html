Ah Ma's story
 Growing up The War Kampung Life Merdeka

I was born in 1930.

Actually I’m also not sure when, we didn’t celebrate birthdays in those days.

My name is Goh Mei Ling. We lived in the Clarke Quay area.

And I had a twin.

My mother couldn’t manage us both, so I was given away to the lady in the next room, who had no children of her own.

But when we were 4, my twin died—so I went back to live with my parents.

Actually we were not a big family. But my family was longing for a boy.

Every time a child was born, they would write back to China for them to pick a name.

But then, after a few weeks, the child would die.

So when the next son, your Lao Gu, was born,
they stopped writing.

They didn’t want to give him a name.

They simply called him Ah Gu, which means “bull”.

In fact, there was a third child, another son.
But one day, the baby got sick and had fever.

Mum refused to take him to the doctor,
saying we had no money and the General Hospital was too far away.

But then the fever didn’t go down,
so in the end she had to take him--

That night, she came back... alone.

I asked her, “Ma, where’s didi?”

She was crying.


“Too late...
The doctor said we came too late.”

When we had time, I went to work in the factory, sorting little plastic toys.

I wanted to study, but girls didn’t study.


My brother had to study, but he didn’t like studying.

So when I was older, a few friends and I
started our own night school.

We got the village school teacher to tutor us.
That was how I learned to read.
