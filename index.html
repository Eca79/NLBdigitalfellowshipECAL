Ah Ma's Story

If you cross the overhead bridge on Ang Mo Kio Avenue 3
and you look up…

There on the fourth floor you may see
curtains billowing gently,
a hint of orchids and bougainvillea,
an elderly lady peering out

Ah Ma!

Eh? Ah Ling, you’re here! Have you eaten?

Yes, I just ate--

You want to eat something? Come, let me cut you some fruit--

But Grandma, I just ate—

Come, come, I’ll cut some oranges…

... ...Oh well.


I should know by now
that food is Grandma's way
of showing love...

Ah Ma’s home is filled with relics from an era past…

a framed photograph of her in her sweet youth…

a seventy-year old Singer sewing machine…

a pink-and-green wire egg basket
that has been sitting atop the fridge
all my growing up years…
